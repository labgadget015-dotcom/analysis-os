## What to Expect in Your Report

Every analysis report follows this structure:

### 1. **Executive Summary** (1 page)
- Your top business question and answer
- 3-5 key findings
- Top recommendations with estimated annual impact
- Recommended next steps

### 2. **Key Metrics Dashboard**
- Current state (baseline)
- Benchmarks (how you compare to competitors/best-in-class)
- Opportunity gap (where improvement is possible)

### 3. **Detailed Findings**
- Data profile (what data we analyzed)
- Key insights by business question
- Supported by data visualizations
- Assumptions documented

### 4. **Actionable Recommendations**

Each recommendation includes:
- **What** to do (specific action)
- **Why** (evidence from data + business logic)
- **Impact** (what metric improves, by how much, $K annual value)
- **Effort** (time and resources required)
- **Timeline** (weeks to implement)
- **Owner** (who is responsible)
- **Success Metrics** (how to measure if it worked)
- **Confidence** (80%, 90%, etc. based on data strength)

### 5. **Next Steps & Roadmap**
- Which recommendations to implement first (prioritized by impact/effort)
- Phased rollout over 12 months
- Dependencies and risks
- Monthly milestones

### 6. **Technical Appendix** (Optional)
- Data quality assessment
- Assumptions and limitations
- Sensitivity analysis (what if things change)
- Additional analysis on request

## How We Ensure Quality

Every analysis goes through our **5-Stage Pipeline**, with validation at each stage:

1. **Data Prep** - Clean, standardize, document your data
2. **Core Analysis** - Answer your key questions with evidence
3. **Drill-down** - Segment analysis to find hidden patterns
4. **Actionization** - Convert findings into specific, measurable recommendations
5. **Iteration** - Validate assumptions, design experiments, check for biases

Additionally:
- **All claims are backed by data** - Every recommendation cites the specific data and calculation
- **Assumptions are documented** - We flag limitations and sensitivity (what if assumptions change)
- **Biases are checked** - We actively look for survivorship bias, selection bias, etc.
- **Results are peer-reviewed** - Senior analysts validate findings before delivery

## Glossary of Key Terms

- **ROI (Return on Investment)** - Dollar value gained / dollar cost = payback ratio
- **Lift** - Percentage improvement (e.g., "5% conversion lift" = 5% more customers convert)
- **Cohort** - A group of customers with something in common (same signup month, plan tier, etc.)
- **Significance** - Statistical confidence that results are real (not due to luck/randomness). We target 95%+ confidence.
- **LTV (Lifetime Value)** - Total profit from a customer over their lifetime
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** - How much you spend to acquire one customer
- **Segmentation** - Dividing customers into groups with distinct characteristics
- **Churn** - Percentage of customers who leave/cancel in a time period
- **Conversion** - Percentage of prospects who become customers
